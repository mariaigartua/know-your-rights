LEARN YOUR RIGHTS

  • You have the right to remain silent. Say: "I choose to remain silent."
  • You have the right to refuse consent for a search without a warrant.
  • You have the right to a lawyer if arrested. Say: "I want a lawyer."
  • You do not need to reveal your immigration status.

Police Encounters

Knowing your rights when interacting with the police is critical. Here’s what you should remember:

  • Right to Remain Silent: You do not have to answer questions beyond identifying yourself. Say clearly, "I wish to remain silent."
  • Refusal of Search: If an officer asks to search your person, car, or home, you can refuse unless they have a warrant.
  • Legal Representation: If arrested, immediately request a lawyer and do not speak further without one.
  • Recording the Encounter: In many places, you have the right to record police interactions, but ensure you do not interfere.
  • Example Scenario: If pulled over, keep hands visible, provide license and registration, and ask if you are free to go. If not, ask for a reason and remain calm.

Workplace Rights

Employees are entitled to safe and fair working conditions. Here’s what you need to know:

  • Protection from Discrimination: Employers cannot discriminate based on race, gender, religion, disability, or other protected statuses.
  • Right to Fair Pay: You have the right to be paid at least minimum wage and overtime for extra hours.
  • Safe Work Environment: If conditions are unsafe, you can report the issue to OSHA without fear of retaliation.
  • Example Scenario: If your employer is not paying for overtime, document your hours, speak to HR, and consider filing a wage claim.

Student Rights

Students have protections that ensure a fair educational environment. These include:

  • Freedom of Expression: You can express opinions as long as they do not disrupt learning.
  • Right to Privacy: School officials cannot search your belongings without reasonable suspicion.
  • Protection Against Bullying: Schools must address bullying and harassment claims.
  • Example Scenario: If a teacher singles you out unfairly due to race or gender, report it to school administration and document incidents.

Immigration Laws

Regardless of status, immigrants have rights that should be understood:

  • Right to Remain Silent: If stopped by ICE, you do not have to answer questions about your immigration status.
  • Home Raids: Do not open the door unless ICE has a judicial warrant.
  • Legal Representation: If detained, request a lawyer and avoid signing documents you do not understand.
  • Example Scenario: If ICE stops you on the street, ask if you are free to leave. If not, state that you want to remain silent and need a lawyer.
